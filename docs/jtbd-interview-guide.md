# Customer Discovery Interview Guide: Buildplane

## Purpose
Reconstruct the timeline of how developers currently use (or fail to use) AI coding agents at scale, to validate and refine the Buildplane job statement.

---

## Part 1: First Thought (When did this become a problem?)

1. Walk me through your current process when you start a new AI coding session. What do you open first? What do you type first?
2. When did you first realize that using multiple AI agents together was harder than you expected?
3. What was happening in your work/life at that moment? What were you trying to build?
4. Had you tried to solve this before? What did you try? What worked? What didn't?
5. What was the exact moment you thought "there has to be a better way to do this"?

**Probe for:**
- Specific projects or deadlines that triggered the search
- Whether the pain was gradual or sudden
- Any workarounds they built (scripts, prompt libraries, tmux setups)

---

## Part 2: Search (What alternatives did they explore?)

6. What tools or approaches did you look into? Claude Code, Cursor, Devin, Aider, custom scripts?
7. What made you keep using what you're using today instead of switching?
8. Did you try any "AI agent orchestration" tools? Which ones? What happened?
9. Who did you talk to about this before settling on your current setup?
10. What almost convinced you to try something completely different?

**Probe for:**
- The specific features or behaviors that ruled options in or out
- Whether they discussed this with colleagues (viral signal)
- Non-obvious alternatives they considered (e.g., "I just hired a junior dev instead")

---

## Part 3: Purchase/Adoption (What tipped the scales?)

11. The last time you installed or committed to a new AI dev tool, walk me through exactly what happened. Where were you? What time of day? What convinced you?
12. What were you most worried about before trying it?
13. What almost stopped you from adopting it?
14. How long did it take to feel like you got value from it?
15. Who else at your company or in your circle uses it?

**Probe for:**
- The exact "Big Hire" moment and what triggered it
- Whether anxiety was about data privacy, workflow disruption, or learning curve
- Social proof or word-of-mouth influence

---

## Part 4: Usage (The Little Hire -- repeated decisions)

16. Today, when you need AI help with code, how do you decide which tool to reach for?
17. Walk me through your last 3 AI coding sessions. Which tool? Why that one? How did it go?
18. When do you abandon the tool and do it yourself instead?
19. What's the most frustrating part of your current AI workflow on a typical day?
20. What would make you stop using your current setup and switch to something else?

**Probe for:**
- The gap between what they hoped the tool would do vs what it actually does
- Specific moments where the tool failed the Little Hire (installed but not used)
- Workarounds, compensating behaviors, and "hacky" solutions

---

## Part 5: Undiscovered Jobs

21. Is there something you wish your AI tools could do together but they currently can't?
22. Are you using spreadsheets, notes, or other tools alongside your AI agents? What for?
23. Have you ever given up on delegating to AI and done the work yourself? What was the task?
24. What's the most time you've wasted switching between tools or re-explaining context in one session?
25. If AI coding agents could perfectly remember everything about your projects, what would you ask them to do differently?

**Probe for:**
- Workarounds that reveal unmet job dimensions
- Emotional pain points (anxiety about losing control, imposter syndrome from using AI)
- Social pressure (feeling like you're "cheating" or feeling like you're falling behind if you don't use AI)

---

## Interview Signals to Track

| Signal | What It Reveals |
|--------|----------------|
| "I built my own script to..." | Unserved job dimension |
| "I just copy-paste context every time" | Memory gap |
| "My team uses different tools" | Coordination problem |
| "I stopped using it after a week" | Little Hire failure |
| "It's fine for simple stuff but..." | Scope limitation |
| "I don't trust it to..." | Core anxiety |
| "I'd use it more if..." | Untapped Little Hire |

---

## Post-Interview Synthesis

After each interview:
1. Write the job statement in the person's own words, not yours
2. Map their biggest Push, Pull, Anxiety, and Habit forces
3. Note the exact moment of Big Hire and Little Hire decisions
4. List non-obvious competitors they mentioned (including "I just do it myself")
5. Flag any workarounds that could be turned into Buildplane features

Run 10+ interviews before drawing conclusions. Look for patterns, not individual preferences.
